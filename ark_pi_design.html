Guide: Dr. Ajit Paranjpe, Ex CTO of Veecco Instruments, US

[Aug'23 - July'24]

Abstract:

This project focused on designing a Mechanical Lift-Off Apparatus to facilitate the transfer of Gallium Nitride (GaN) layers from sapphire wafers using Controlled Spalling Technology. The apparatus was engineered to deliver precise force, temperature control, and motion guidance to enable defect-free layer release, ensuring scalability for next-generation semiconductor manufacturing.

Project Details:

The Controlled Spalling Process relies on the careful detachment of a thin GaN layer from a sapphire substrate using a stressor layer and adhesive tape. The designed apparatus integrates mechanical, thermal, and fluid systems to carry out this process reliably.

Apparatus Features:
  • Roller Application Mechanism
    Inspired by laminators, the roller ensures that adhesive polyimide tape is applied uniformly across the wafer surface.
    Controlled rolling minimizes air gaps, uneven adhesion, and potential wafer cracks.
  • Adjustable Downforce System
    Integrated with spring-based and screw-based adjustments to finely tune bonding pressure.
    Prevents over-compression, which could cause wafer fracture, and under-compression, which could lead to incomplete bonding.
  • Cartridge Heater Press
    Provides localized heating (up to ~80 °C) to the bonding interface.
    Improves adhesion between the GaN stack and the carrier substrate during the bonding stage.
  • Water Cooling System
    Actively cools the bonded wafer-carrier setup down to ~15 °C.
    Cooling strengthens adhesion while reducing thermal stress on the brittle GaN layers.
  • Guiding Frame & Locus Control
    Ensures that the slider follows a pre-defined motion path.
    Maintains stability and alignment during the gradual peeling of the GaN layer.
Step-by-Step Process of Controlled Spalling:
  1. Preparation of the Wafer
    A sapphire wafer with a GaN epitaxial stack is coated with a nickel stressor layer.
    This stressor layer helps initiate and guide the fracture along the desired depth within the GaN.
  2. Application of Polyimide Tape
    Using the roller mechanism, polyimide adhesive tape is uniformly applied over the wafer.
    The tape must adhere firmly to the stressor layer to ensure a controlled lift-off.
  3. Initiation of Spalling
    A mechanical slider applies a carefully controlled peeling force at the wafer edge.
    The guiding frame ensures that this force follows a stable locus path, initiating a fracture at the GaN-nickel interface.
  4. Controlled Layer Release
    The peeling action continues gradually, propagating the fracture across the wafer.
    The result is the detachment of a thin GaN layer from the sapphire, now carried on the polyimide tape.
  5. Carrier Substrate Bonding
    The GaN layer (still attached to the tape) is bonded onto a disposable carrier substrate.
    The cartridge heater press applies both heat (~80 °C) and pressure to enhance bonding.
  6. Cooling Phase
    A water cooling system lowers the bonded assemblyâ€™s temperature to ~15 °C.
    This step strengthens adhesion and prevents warping or cracking.
  7. Tape Removal
    Once cooled, the polyimide tape is carefully peeled off.
    The GaN layer remains securely attached to the disposable carrier substrate.
Conclusion:

The designed apparatus successfully addresses the challenges of wafer fragility, bonding uniformity, and thermal stress in spalling. By integrating precise roller mechanics, adjustable pressure control, localized heating, and active cooling, the system ensures repeatable and scalable GaN exfoliation. This innovation contributes to advancing GaN-based semiconductor technology, which promises superior performance compared to silicon in high-power and high-frequency applications.
