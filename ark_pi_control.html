Guide: Dr. Ajit Paranjpe, Ex CTO of Veecco Instruments, US

[May'23 - July'23]

Abstract:

This project focused on the development of a Level-3 Autonomous Vehicle Control Framework, integrating steering, traction, and regenerative braking systems. The aim was to ensure accurate trajectory tracking, stability across different driving conditions, and improved energy efficiency through regenerative braking.

Project Details:
  • Steering Control: Evaluated Pure Pursuit, Stanley, and Model Predictive Control (MPC). A hybrid of Stanley and MPC was implemented, achieving high accuracy with a maximum trajectory deviation of just 0.97 m, below the 1.12 m limit.
  • Traction Control: Designed to monitor wheel speeds and adjust motor torque to prevent skidding or loss of control. The model ensured consistent velocity tracking and optimized battery performance.
  • Regenerative Braking: Developed a PI controller-based regenerative braking model that recaptured braking energy and fed it back into the battery. Results showed measurable improvements in battery state of charge (SOC).
Conclusion:

The final framework successfully integrated all three modules, enabling precise path tracking, enhanced stability, and sustainable energy use. The project demonstrated how hybrid control systems can improve the robustness and efficiency of autonomous electric vehicles.

Skills Obtained:
  • Control system design and implementation in MATLAB Simulink
  • Knowledge of MPC, Stanley, and Pure Pursuit Controllers
  • Vehicle dynamics modeling and hybrid control strategy development
  • Energy optimization techniques in EVs (traction and regenerative braking systems)
